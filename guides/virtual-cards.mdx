# <div className="subtitle"><span>How It Works</span></div>

## <div><span>1. Card Issuance</span></div>

A virtual card is generated instantly via Ramp’s API, optionally configured with:

<ul className="lists">
  <li>
    <b>Merchant restrictions</b> – limiting transactions to specific providers
    (such as airlines or hotels)
  </li>
  <li>
    <b>Spending caps</b> – ensuring charges do not exceed predefined thresholds
  </li>
  <li>
    <b>Expiration dates</b> – to auto-expire unused cards for security and
    refund alignment
  </li>
</ul>

## <div><span>2. Payment Execution</span></div>

The generated card details (PAN, CVV, expiration) are securely delivered via Ramp’s Vault API. You pass these details to your vendor, travel partner, or supplier to process payment.

## <div><span>3. Reconciliation</span></div>

<ul className="lists">
  <li>
    <b>Card ID → Transaction mapping</b> makes it easy to automate
    reconciliation
  </li>
  <li>
    <b>Supports adjustments & refunds</b> even after the card is locked or
    expired
  </li>
  <li>
    <b>Webhooks are supported</b> for notifications when a card is used,
    declined, or refunded
  </li>
</ul>
