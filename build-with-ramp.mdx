# <div className="subtitle"><span>What the Partnership Looks Like</span></div>

Building an integration with Ramp means entering a partnership focused on delivering a high-quality experience to our shared customers. Each side plays a clear role in making that happen.

<div className="spacer-small">
  <b>Your side:</b>
</div>

<ul className="lists">
  <li>
    <b>Pricing and monetization</b> — You set your pricing, bill customers
    directly, and keep 100% of revenue. Ramp does not process payments or
    participate in billing.
  </li>
  <li>
    <b>Building and hosting your integration</b> — You develop and maintain your
    integration using Ramp's APIs.
  </li>
  <li>
    <b>Customer support</b> — You assist customers through your own support
    channels and provide a public support email.
  </li>
  <li>
    <b>Documentation</b> — You host setup guides, workflow instructions, and
    troubleshooting on your site.
  </li>
  <li>
    <b>Marketing assets</b> — You supply the logo, descriptions, screenshots,
    and videos for your listing.
  </li>
</ul>

<div className="spacer-small">
  <b>Ramp's side:</b>
</div>

<ul className="lists">
  <li>
    <b>API troubleshooting & advisory</b> — Our team helps you debug API issues
    and offers guidance on best practices and specific use cases.
  </li>
  <li>
    <b>Product review and guidance</b> — We review your integration flows and
    provide feedback. Approval is required before production access.
  </li>
  <li>
    <b>Distribution</b> — Your integration reaches 50,000+ businesses once
    approved.
  </li>
</ul>
This partnership model gives you full ownership of your product while Ramp
provides oversight, guidance, and reach.

# Pricing Guidance (Optional)

You control your pricing. Ramp does not charge fees or take a revenue share. Many integrations are free, while others charge for their services—the choice is yours.

Best practices for pricing:

<ul className="lists">
  <li>Price relative to the value your integration delivers to customers.</li>
  <li>Keep pricing predictable and easy to understand.</li>
  <li>
    Factor in implementation cost, ongoing maintenance, and support burden.
  </li>
</ul>

Common pricing models include:

<ul className="lists">
  <li>Flat annual subscription.</li>
  <li>FImplementation fee plus annual renewal.</li>
  <li>FUsage-based pricing (for example, per transaction or per bill).</li>
  <li>FTiered feature bundles based on functionality.</li>
</ul>

# Writing Great Documentation

High-quality documentation improves customer activation and reduces support load. It is also heavily used by Ramp's sales, customer success, and support teams when discussing your integration.

Your documentation should:

<ul className="lists">
  <li>Be publicly accessible and hosted on your website.</li>
  <li>Include prerequisites for both Ramp and your platform.</li>
  <li>Provide step-by-step setup instructions.</li>
  <li>Detail key user workflows and common use cases.</li>
  <li>Offer troubleshooting tips and a frequently asked questions section.</li>
</ul>

# Security Requirements

Partners must fully complete security disclosures and provide:

<ul className="lists">
  <li>
    Security certifications and/or attestations such as SOC 2 Type II, ISO
    27001, PCI, or a recent third-party penetration test.{" "}
  </li>
  <li>
    Full certification and/or pen test reports (not just summary letters).
  </li>
  <li>A public URL to your security policy.</li>
  <li>A dedicated and monitored security contact email address.</li>
  <li>
    Written descriptions covering:
    <ul>
      <li>What data your integration processes and how it is secured. </li>
      <li>Where and how data is stored. </li>
      <li>Your data retention policy. </li>
      <li>
        Whether and how employees at your company can access customer data.
      </li>
    </ul>
  </li>
</ul>

Early-stage companies may use a recent third-party pen test in place of full SOC 2 or ISO 27001 certifications, subject to Ramp's review.

# Support Expectations

You own first-line support for your integration. Ramp will route customer questions about your integration to you.

<ul className="lists">
  <li>
    Provide a public support email address; this will be visible to customers
    and used by them to troubleshoot issues.
  </li>
  <li>
    Maintain clear customer-facing documentation for setup, key workflows, and
    troubleshooting.
  </li>
  <li>
    Provide an internal support guide for Ramp teams that explains how to triage
    issues, what information to collect, and how to escalate to your team.
  </li>
</ul>
